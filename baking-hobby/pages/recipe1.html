Castella Cake

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C and prepare boiling water for the water bath (around 70°C)
  2. Line your baking tin with parchment paper on the bottom and the side in 2 sections separately. Make sure the side strip section is taller than your tin a bit allowing you to pull the cake out of the tin later after baking.
  3. Sift and combine the flour (71g) and cornstarch (29g) in a bowl.
  4. Separate the egg yolk (6) and egg white (6) into separate bowls.
  5. Melt and stir the butter in the milk in a saucepan at around 70°C .
  6. Immediately after the milk and butter are fully emulsified, add it to your flour mixture and whisk well until well combined. Then add the egg yolks (6) to the batter and whisk well until well combined.
  7. Using a stand mixer/electric mixer beat the egg whites at low speed and add cream of tartar (½ tsp) after the egg whites have turned foamy. Then gradually add the sugar (100g) to the egg whites as it is whisked. Once all the sugar has been added you can increase the speed to high and whisk until you achieve stiff peaks.
  8. Fold ¼ of your egg whites into the batter until well combined. Then pour the batter into your egg whites and fold until well combined.
  9. Pour your finished batter into the baking tin evenly, then use a skewer to help spread the batter evenly and get rid of air bubbles within the batter.
  10. Put the baking tin in the water bath and put it in the oven and bake for 45-55mins.
  11. Take the cake out of the tin and let it cool for 5-10mins and it is ready to be served when warm.

Ingredients

  • 71g Plain Flour
  • 29g Corn Flour
  • 100g Unsalted Butter (Can be substituted with other oil like Corn Oil)
  • 100g Milk
  • 6 Large Egg Yolks
  • 6 Large Egg Whites
  • 1/2 tsp Cream of Tartar
  • 100g Caster Sugar
